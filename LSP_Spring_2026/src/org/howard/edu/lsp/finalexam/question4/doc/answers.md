At the start of the semester, I mainly saw AI as a source of quick answers. Since then, my perspective has become more balanced. I have come to realize that I find AI most valuable when it supports me, rather than replacing me. For instance, I now use AI to generate JavaDocs, review code, verify solutions against requirements, and enhance them until needed to assess its suggestions, compare them to assignment criteria, and determine their accuracy. This experience showed me that while AI is helpful, it still requires human judgment.

In future programming and coursework, I plan to use AI for code review, debugging, documentation, and brainstorming design explanations. I will also use it to ask questions when I am stuck, but I do not intend to rely on it for complete solutions. Instead, I want to use AI as a learning tool to deepen my understanding, catch mistakes, and write clearer code.
